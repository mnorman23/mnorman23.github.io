Suction Station

Interdisciplinary Design Project, September 2025-March 2026.

Our client, the Morton Arboretum, approached us with a problem. They were two years behind sorting leaf litter for climate change research. Our job? Find a way to make the sorting the small pieces more efficient for their volunteers.

We first determined a journey map of this process. Looking at the entire journey of the leaf litter from dirt to analysis, we found the biggest slowdown came from the manual sorting process.

We designed and built a few low fidelity mockups and received feedback from our client and professionals in various industries.

Halfway through, we ended up choosing to go all in on the suction station. This is because our users and client preferred using it over other ideas.

A few more rounds of iteration and testing later...

...leads to our final design!

Minecraft Steve

Machining, January-March 2026

The prompt: in a team, a group designs a minimum two part piece that comes together. My team opted to make a figurine of Minecraft Steve with opposible limbs.

So we did the college student thing and made an ambitious, 8-piece structure.

The initial sketches below used a grid to ensure proportionality to the actual Minecraft Steve model. Since Steve's arms only rotated along one axis, we opted out of doing a ball joint and did an easier fit model.

This design underwent multiple revisions.

Initially, we did not understand requirements for injection molding. Parts needed to be hollow. This complicated our design well past the initial 4 part mold.

Our second design dealt with machinability constraints. Clearly, we still had a lot to learn. We also realized that using symmetry would only need to create four unique molds for tooling. This meant we could make our parts bigger to use more mold space and reduce tooling time.

More teamwork, more design edits later, and our molds were finally finished. Now, off to the CAM process.

Umbrella-Froggy Rainboots

Industrial Design, March 2025

The prompt? Make an inventive pair of footwear. Immediately, I wanted to design a cute pair of rainboots.

When I think of rain, I think of puddles, mud, and everything frogs. I asked myself, do froggy rainboots exist already? I was disappointed to find every pair of froggy rainboots were creepy or poor quality.

I made it my job to do something about this injustice.

I went through a lot of ideation with my thumbnails and initial sketches. First drafts ranged from exagerrated cuteness to high fashion. After receiving feedback from my professor and peers, I further narrowed my designs to a second draft.

With these designs, I ended up going with the umbrella design. I further explored its shape and found the shape of an upside down umbrella to find it looked like a flower. So I turned it into a water lily when folded up.

Based on more feedback and refinement, this was the final design!

Based off of feedback from hummingbird enthusiasts in my area, my peers, and further research, I sketched out more defined drafts.

At the end, I created my final drafts in full color.
